# Three Siblings and the Shadow Over Hogwarts
### Starring Maria (13), Pepe (12), and Luna (9)
### with a little help from Harry, Ron & Hermione

The thunder cracked. The lights went dim.
A dusty book — the cover grim —
lay open on the attic floor.
Maria poked it. "Just a bore."

But Pepe, twelve, read out the words
(in Latin, backwards — fast, absurd),
and Luna touched the glowing page…
WHOOSH! — like a bird freed from its cage,

they tumbled, spun, then THUMP, THUMP, THUMP —
three siblings landed in a clump
on cold stone steps in cold stone halls,
with cobwebbed corners, creepy walls.

"Where ARE we?" Pepe gasped for air.
A sign read: HOGWARTS. ENTER IF YOU DARE.
The torches flickered, low and blue.
Maria yanked her phone out. "…No new

texts? No bars? No signal? NONE?!"
She shook it. "This is so not fun."
A voice said, "Phones won't help you here."
Three kids spun 'round — and froze in fear —

then smiled. For there, by candlelight,
stood three young wizards, wands held tight:
a boy with glasses, scar, and frown,
a red-haired boy, a girl with crown
of bushy curls and clever eyes.
"I'm Harry." "Ron." "Hermione — wise
enough to know we're in a mess.
The castle's cursed. Can you guess?
Some dark and ancient spell's awake,
and every monster, ghoul, and snake
is loose tonight. We have to fight
to save the school before first light!"

---

"This way!" cried Ron — but down they slid
into a pit. (Bad call there, kid.)
A vine shot up! It grabbed Pepe's knee —
a plant with teeth?! "GET OFF OF ME!"

"DON'T struggle!" Hermione called out clear.
"It's Devil's Snare — it feeds on fear!
Relax! Go limp!" They all went slack…
and FWOOMP — the plant just dropped them back.

A BOOM. A BOOM. The floorboards shook.
Around the corner — one good look —
a TROLL! Ten feet! With club and stink!
It swung! They ducked! It crashed! Big think:

"Distract it!" Harry yelled the plan.
So Pepe — brave (and starving) — ran
and lobbed his last and only snack.
The troll turned, sniffed… then thundered back!

It tripped on Ron's good Wingardium —
the club swung down — and bopped the cranium
of its own lumpy troll-ish head.
THUD. Down it went. "…Is it dead?"

"Just napping," Hermione said with care.
"Now RUN — there's MUCH worse waiting there."

---

The air went cold. The torches died.
A hooded shape began to glide —
no face, just breath, just dread, just doom.
Three Dementors filled the room.

Poor Luna shivered. "I feel so sad…
like every happy thought's gone bad."
"That's what they DO!" cried Harry. "Think
of something JOYFUL — quick! — don't blink!"

He raised his wand: "EXPECTO —" crack! —
"PATRONUM!" Light came beating back
the dark — a glowing silver stag!
The Dementors fled. (No time to brag.)

But Luna — brave and small and bright —
had thought of GELATO, pure delight,
and from her hands (no wand, just glee)
a tiny silver fox broke free!

The wizards gasped. "She's got the spark!"
"A NATURAL!" "Even in the dark!"
And Luna grinned, no longer scared:
"I think… I think I'm rather prepared."

---

Down corridors of web and gloom
came skittering legs — clack-clack — KA-BOOM —
a SPIDER big as Pepe's bed!
("I'm done. I'm out. I'm leaving," he said.)

"They fear the fire!" Ron cried. "Be quick —
a torch! Grab one! The nearest stick!"
Maria, queen of being cool,
seized flame and roared, "BACK OFF, you ghoul!"

She swung. The spiders shrieked and fled.
"…Did Maria just DO that?" Pepe said.
She flipped her hair. "I mean. I tried.
Don't make it weird." (She glowed with pride.)

---

At last they reached the chamber's core,
a vast and echoing marble floor,
where coiled in shadow — fang and scale —
the BASILISK began to wail:

a serpent long as any train,
whose stare alone could melt your brain.
"DON'T LOOK!" yelled Harry. "Eyes shut TIGHT!
We win this one by sound, not sight!"

So eyes-shut, ears-wide, hand-in-hand,
the six became one fearless band.
Hermione whispered, calm and clear:
"It guards the spell. The curse is here."

Ron clattered shields and clanging pots;
the serpent lunged at all the spots.
While Harry climbed (eyes shut — so brave!),
bold Pepe (starving) up and gave

a mighty roar: "OVER HERE, you snake!"
(He'd grabbed a chicken leg for bait.)
The serpent smashed straight through the wall —
then Luna's fox-light blinded all —

Maria hurled the troll's old club,
Hermione cast the final rub
of magic — "FINITE!" — bright and grand —
the CURSE dissolved across the land!

The serpent crumbled, turned to stone.
The castle sighed. The torches shone.
The sad gray gloom rolled back like tide —
and Hogwarts WOKE: alive inside!

---

The ghosts all cheered. The portraits roared.
The Great Hall's doors flew wide toward
a feast — oh, PEPE'S kind of feast! —
with pies and roasts and cakes (at least

a hundred)! "Eat! You've earned the lot!"
said Ron. And Pepe ate the plot —
clean through three plates. Maria smiled:
no phone, no pose — just like a child.

And Luna, hero of the night,
made foxes dance from pure delight.
"You three could STAY," said Harry, kind.
"We'd train you up. We wouldn't mind!"

But just then — WHOOSH! — the book reglowed.
"That's us," sighed Pepe. "Time we owed
the real world one." They hugged the three.
"Come back!" "We will — eventually!"

WHOOSH! — they spun and THUMP, THUMP, THUMP —
back to the attic, in a clump.
The dusty book snapped shut. All still.
Maria breathed. "…Okay. That was kind of brill."

Pepe burped. (A noble sound.)
Then Luna made a fox spin 'round —
a tiny silver one, that stayed.
"…Guys," Luna whispered, unafraid,

"I think we kept a little spark."
And in that dim and dusty dark,
three siblings grinned, all fear long gone —
the bravest team that Hogwarts won.
