# Three Siblings Loose in Rome
### Starring Maria (13), Pepe (12), and Luna (9)

The plane went *BUMP* on Roman ground,
With pigeons, scooters, noise all 'round.
Three siblings tumbled out the door —
Italia! Pizza! Ruins! And MORE!

Maria, thirteen, queen of cool,
Declared the whole trip "boring, drool."
But snapped four hundred photos, though —
"Just for my feed. You'll never know."

Pepe, twelve, had one demand:
A slice of pizza in each hand.
"I read the guidebook!" Pepe said.
(He'd only read the part marked BREAD.)

And Luna, nine, eyes big and round,
Heard every echo, every sound.
"Is Rome," she whispered, "full of GHOSTS?
Of emperors and creepy hosts?"

---

First stop: the mighty Colosseum —
("Two thousand years!" "Can we PLEASE see 'em?")
Pepe roared and raised his fist:
"I'd WIN! I'd be the best! Gladiatist!"

He flexed, he posed, he took a swing,
Then tripped face-first on a stony ring.
Maria filmed it — "Best clip yet!" —
While Luna fed a bird her baguette.

Next, the Forum, grand and vast,
Where mighty Caesar walked the past.
"It's ROCKS," moaned Pepe. "Rocks and weeds.
Where's lunch? A boy has lunching needs."

To the Trevi Fountain, blue and bright —
Toss a coin and you'll come back, right?
Pepe tossed his coin with flair…
then tossed his *wallet.* (It's still there.)

The Spanish Steps! All hundred-plus.
"WHY are there STAIRS?!" began the fuss.
But at the top — oh, sweet salvation:
GELATO! Cause for celebration!

Luna picked one scoop of blue.
Maria, three. Then asked for two.
Pepe got six (plus extra cone).
It melted faster than his moan.

It dripped! It ran! It hit his shoe!
A pigeon swooped and stole some too!
And Luna laughed so hard she fell
into a fountain. Ah, well. Well well.

---

The Pantheon — that ancient dome,
with one round hole to call its own.
"It's so the GODS can watch us pray."
"Or so the RAIN can wreck your day."

And right on cue — a clap of thunder!
Rain poured straight down through that wonder.
Three soggy kids, one ancient floor.
Maria filmed it. Got some more.

The Mouth of Truth! "Now place your hand —
if you have LIED, it BITES. So grand."
Poor Luna shook. Pepe grinned wide,
stuck in his hand… then SCREAMED, and lied:

"IT GOT ME! AHHH! It bit clean through!"
(It didn't. But the whole crowd flew.)
Maria sighed, "You're such a pain."
(She'd jumped a foot. She won't explain.)

To Vatican, so high, so grand,
where painted ceilings make you stand
and crane your neck right at the top —
"My NECK!" cried Pepe. "Make it STOP!"

Maria stared, and dropped her pose:
"That's… honestly… amazing," — froze —
then caught herself with cool-girl frown:
"I mean. It's fine. Can we sit down?"

---

At Piazza Navona, lit and loud,
with statues, fountains, evening crowd,
a painter sketched the three for free…
then charged them forty euros. Whee.

They ate. Oh, how those siblings ate!
Spaghetti piled on every plate,
cacio e pepe, pizza, bread —
then Pepe asked for MORE instead.

Footsore, full, and sunburned pink,
too tired now to even think,
they wobbled home through golden light:
three little Romans, quite a sight.

In bed, Maria scrolled her phone —
four hundred pics of Rome, well-shown.
"Best trip ever," she let slip…
then, quick: "Don't TELL. I'll deny it. Zip."

Pepe dreamed of pizza skies,
of gladiators, fries, more fries.
And Luna, brave through ghost and gloom,
asked sleepily across the room:

"Can we come back?" The answer? "Sì!"
(Even Maria — secretly.)
For Rome had won, beyond all doubt,
three little hearts it won't let out.
